Gabrielle 'Coco' Chanel

Fashion designer & businesswoman

Coco Chanel wearing one of her designs

Gabrielle Bonheur ‘Coco’ Chanel was a French fashion designer and businesswoman. The founder of the Chanel brand, she was credited in the post-World War I era with popularising a casual chic as the feminine standard of style. This replaced the ‘corseted silhouette’ that was dominant beforehand with a style that was simpler, far less time consuming to put on and remove, more comfortable, and less expensive, all without sacrificing elegance. She is the only fashion designer listed on Time magazine's list of the 100 most influential people of the 20th century.

'Fashion changes, but style endures.'

A prolific fashion creator, Chanel extended her influence beyond couture clothing, realising her aesthetic design in jewellery, handbags, and fragrance. Her signature scent, Chanel No. 5, has become an iconic product, and Chanel herself designed her famed interlocked-CC monogram, which has been in use since the 1920s.

In the 1920s she introduced the Little Black Dress to fashion. Intended to be affordable and easy-to-wear. Vogue rightly predicted that it would be worn around the world.

Chanel became very rich and famous during her life, but her childhood was a humble one. She was the daughter of a peasant and a street vendor and was born in a poorhouse.

'There are people who have money and people who are rich.'

Known all around the world as Coco Chanel, this wasn't her original name. Born Gabrielle Chanel, she became ‘Coco’ after being given the nickname by soldiers in the audience while singing on stage. It means something like darling.

'My life didn't please me, so I created one that did.'

Coco Chanel was unapologetic in her choices. She was accused of being a collaborator during the war because she made circumstances work for her. There is no doubt she was highly resourceful.

'I don't spend time beating on a wall, hoping to transform it into a door.'

She demonstrated that you can have both elegance and practicality. Two qualities I value in my own work as a web designer.'

'Some people think luxury is the opposite of poverty. It is not. It is the opposite of vulgarity.'

Chanel bag

She overturned traditional views about femininity. Championing what was by some called a ‘boyish’ look.

As far as I can see she didn’t set out to ‘change the world’ but by sticking to her own style and vision - by creating her own life - she also became hugely influential and successful. For me, the latter is the icing on the cake. Being yourself … is the cake.

On 10 January 1971, after returning from a walk with her friend, Coco died on her bed in the Hotel Ritz. Her last words to her maid Celine were:

'You see, this is how you die.'
